About

What this app is

A simple tool for parents and carers to record their child's ketones, glucose, and seizures during ketogenic diet therapy for epilepsy. It generates summaries that can be shared with your dietitian or neurologist at clinic appointments.

What it isn't

KetoCare doesn't give medical advice. It doesn't tell you when to adjust your child's diet, when to give rescue medication, or when to seek urgent care. Those decisions belong with your child's clinical team and your written care plan.

How to use it

Logging measurements

Tap Log measurement to record a blood ketone or glucose reading. If you enter both blood ketone and glucose, the GKI is calculated automatically.

Logging seizures

Tap Log seizure to start a timer when a seizure begins, and tap Stop when it ends. You can then add the type, possible triggers, and any rescue medication given. If the seizure has already happened, tap Manual entry instead.

History and trends

History shows everything you've logged, day by day. Tap any entry to edit it. Trends shows charts and statistics — daily for short ranges, weekly for a month or two, and monthly for longer periods. From there, Patterns goes deeper, including a type-by-month table of seizures.

Sharing with your dietitian

Export lets you download a Summary report (PDF, good for clinic) or Detailed records (spreadsheet, good for closer review). You can email these or share them however you'd normally share documents.

Settings

Set your child's name, target ranges, and alert thresholds in Settings. These are usually agreed with your dietitian. KetoCare doesn't send reminder notifications — browsers can't reliably wake the app in the background, so most parents set a phone alarm for ketone checks.

In an emergency

Follow your child's emergency care plan. If you don't have one, or you're unsure what to do, contact your local emergency services straight away.

Your data

Everything you log stays on this device. Nothing is uploaded, sent, or shared automatically. The only way data leaves your phone is when you choose to export it (as a PDF, spreadsheet, or backup file) and share it yourself.

If you delete this app, or clear your browser data, the entries are gone. Use the Backup option periodically if you want to be able to restore your records on a new device.

KD
Ketogenic diet.
GKI
Glucose-Ketone Index. Glucose ÷ blood ketone (both mmol/L). A lower number means deeper ketosis relative to glucose.
AM / PM
Readings or events grouped by time of day. AM = midnight to noon; PM = noon to midnight.
mmol/L
Millimoles per litre. The unit used for blood ketone (β-hydroxybutyrate) and blood glucose in the UK and most of Europe.
Classical 4:1 / 3:1 / 2:1
Classical ketogenic diet, by ratio of fat to (protein + carbohydrate) by weight.
MCT
Medium-chain triglyceride ketogenic diet.
MKD
Modified ketogenic diet (UK terminology).
MAD
Modified Atkins diet (US/literature term). Not strictly equivalent to MKD.
LGIT
Low Glycaemic Index Treatment.

KetoCare · Version 1.5

v1.5

  • Monthly view, like a clinic spreadsheet. Trends and Patterns now show calendar months as columns (Apr, May, Jun…) for ranges longer than two months, and Mon–Sun weeks for ranges in between. Short ranges still show daily bars.
  • "Since KD" range. Set when the ketogenic diet started in Settings, and a new chip lets you view everything from that date to now in one go. The export defaults to this range too.
  • Seizure types as a table. The Patterns screen now shows a type-by-month table of frequency and median duration, rather than mini-charts. Matches what dietitians and neurologists are used to reading at clinic.
  • New ranges 6m and 1y on Trends and Patterns. "30d" is now labelled "1m". Custom date range available on both screens.
  • Excel: new Monthly tab right after Summary — types as rows, months as columns, ketones / glucose / GKI / rescue counts summarised underneath. Mirrors the layout that clinicians find easy to read.
  • Abbreviations explained on the About page.
  • Date of birth no longer asked for — KD start date is what clinical conversations actually use.
  • Urine ketone option removed (blood ketone is what clinical decisions use).
